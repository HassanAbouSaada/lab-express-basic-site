About Norman Foster

Norman Robert Foster, Baron Foster of Thames Bank, is a British architect and designer who is considered one of the most important architects of his generation. He has designed iconic buildings all around the world, including the Bank of China Tower in Hong Kong, the Reichstag Building in Berlin, and the Hearst Tower in New York.

Foster was born in Manchester, England in 1935. He studied architecture at the University of Manchester and then at Yale University in the United States on a Henry Fellowship.

After graduating from Yale in 1962, Foster established his own architectural firm, Foster Associates, which later became Foster + Partners. The firm is known for its innovative, sustainable designs and has won numerous awards for its buildings.

Foster was awarded the Pritzker Architecture Prize in 1999 and was given a life peerage in 1999 in recognition of his services to architecture. He is one of the most influential architects of our time and continues to be a driving force in the field.
